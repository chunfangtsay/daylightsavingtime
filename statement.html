Time Difference, Mapping Error is an ongoing project that comes from a series of video installations. It is a journal on the inprogress and the provisional, on ideas and practices of the creative process.

We usually recognize time as cohesive and linear; what if it’s organic? At the opening of the movie Eternity and a Day, an old man said: time is a child who plays a dice on the shore. The boundary of time is inevitably subjective; the limit of measurement is something more organic. I try to redefine time with more intuitive and fictional forms based on my cross-cultural living experience. Lots of things are universal, but different in details on the map. After a long haul flight, an inner clock is still ticking from a parallel time zone.

The final work will be showcased in ArtEZ Finals, July 2026. Tryout with multi-media and mixed materials will be updated every week.
